I went into a music store in Georgia when I was 18 years old. I had saved up $400 from my birthday and Christmas and was going to buy a better Electric guitar. I went to a store in Fayetteville. I walked in and saw this Ibanez Artist and thought it was badass. I asked the owner of I could play it. I plugged it in and kind of quietly ran it through whatever paces I knew to do at the time. I played all the riffs I knew in a distorted sound and strummed some chords with it clean sounding. I then went outside and asked my mom for another $400 so I could buy this $800 guitar. She said no way José.

When I went back in, another kid was playing the guitar. He was about 11 years old. It was like seeing someone holding hands with your girlfriend. I walked around the store wasting time until this punk kid would let me play this guitar one more time before I left without buying anything. He sat the guitar down on the hanger haphazardly and it fell off the wall and knocked a big chip out of it. The kid ran out of the store and my next words were, "How much for the guitar now?” I ran outside and told my mom the story while gasping for air with excitement. I calmed myself down and then said "He would sell the guitar for $600 now.
